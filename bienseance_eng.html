Rules of etiquette

To eat properly

    To eat properly in Japan is art of its own right so here are a few rules to follow before you leave for Japan:
  • Before eating, say “itadakimasu” and after finishing your meal say “gochisosama desu.”
  • When eating with a group, do not start eating until everyone has been served. Also ask if you can pick the last piece from a common dish.
  • It is also frowned upon not to finish your dish and to ask for leftovers to take away.


Finally, avoid eating while walking when you are outside and stand to the side of the sidewalk to not disturb the others.

Japanese manners

In Japan, you will certainly take public transportation so here is some rules and reminder of good behaviour:
Although it is perfectly acceptable to speak in a faint voice on the train or bus, talking loudly and on the phone is generally frowned upon.
Good manners dictate that you queue alongside the train to let passengers out before entering, and that you leave your seat free for the elderly, pregnant women, and people with disabilities.
Finally, in Japan, if you are not in a hurry, you should get on the left-hand side of the escalator!

Here is some information on Japanese manners in general:

  • In most cases, a tilt of the head will be enough to say hello. The Japanese are not very tactile when it comes to everyday exchanges, so avoid kissing or hugging!
  • When exchanging something, always give or receive with both hands. You can say “arigato gozaimasu” to thank someone more frankly.
  • Finally, “Sumimasen,” which generally means “sorry,” can also be used to say, “excuse me” or even “thank you,” depending on the context.

Before leaving for Japan, it is best to know these little rules before being surprised and/or disappointed during your trip:

  • Tattoos are frowned upon in Japan, as they have long been synonymous with criminality and exclusion from society, notably due to the Yakuza. The access to certain onsen (public thermal baths) is forbidden to those with tattoos, no matter how small.
  • We never say “bless you” when someone sneezes, and we do not blow our nose in public. In Japan, people do not hesitate to sniff, even loudly!
  • We never hold the door for people behind us, otherwise we would be doing it all day long.
  • Finally, keep your waste with you, as there are no trash cans in the street anyway!
